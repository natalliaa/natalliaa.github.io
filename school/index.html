Fascinating Facts

South America is the world’s fourth largest continent by area. It is divided into twelve countries: Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Guyana, Paraguay, Peru, Suriname, Uruguay and Venezuela. To the east of the continent is the Atlantic Ocean, and to the west is the Pacific Ocean.

Interesting facts:

  • Number of countries: 12.
  • Population: about 422 million (2016).
  • Largest country: Brazil.
  • Smallest Country: Suriname.
  • Largest city: Sao Paolo in Brazil.
  • Languages: Spanish, Portuguese, and many others.
  • Largest river: The Amazon. It is the worlds' largest river by volume.
  • Largest lake: Lake Titicaca. It is located on the border of Peru and Bolivia.
  • Highest mountain: Cerro Aconcagua. It is located in the Andes Mountains in Argentina.
  • Tallest waterfall: The Angel Falls in Venezuela is the world's tallest waterfall.
  • Largest rainforest: the Amazon jungle is the largest tropical rainforest in the world.
  • Driest place: the Atacama desert in Chile is the driest place on earth.

Source: Britannica Kids, Wikipedia
